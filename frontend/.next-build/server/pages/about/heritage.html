Our Heritage

1,000+ years of Ethiopian coffee culture

From Kaffa to the modern ceremony room, Ethiopian coffee is a social bond, a spiritual pause, and a national inheritance.

The Legend of Kaldi

The story that still introduces the world to Ethiopian coffee

Around 850 AD, legend says a goat herder named Kaldi noticed his goats dancing after eating bright red coffee cherries. That image still captures the wonder of discovery and the energy of the coffee plant itself.

Whether history or myth, the story reflects something true: coffee is inseparable from Ethiopia's landscape and imagination.

Coffee Ceremony

The heart of Ethiopian hospitality

Washing and roasting

Abol

The first round begins with rinsed green beans, roasting over flame, and the aroma filling the room before the first pour.

Grinding by hand

Tona

The second round leans into the texture of mortar and pestle, where the sound of grinding becomes part of the ritual itself.

Brewing in the jebena

Beraka

The final blessing is poured from the jebena into small cups, often alongside popcorn, incense, and long conversation.

Regional Traditions

Different regions, different ceremony accents

Harar

Coffee with spice notes, ceremony drama, and long hospitality traditions.

Sidama

Leaf-lined serving and clean, fruit-led cups that feel bright and celebratory.

Jimma

Coffee sometimes paired with honey and fuller-bodied village-style preparation.

Gondar

Spiced coffee customs with cinnamon and ginger in more festive contexts.
