Our Process

From cherry to cup, the Kaloss way

We design the process around flavor clarity, respectful sourcing, and the discipline Ethiopian coffee deserves.

Step By Step

A vertical journey from harvest to final bag

01
Gala

Handpicking

Only ripe red cherries are selected, often across several passes through the same trees.

02
Tefaye

Sorting and floating

Density checks separate stronger candidates for specialty preparation from lower-value material.

03
Washed, natural, honey

Processing

Each process changes the cup, from floral clarity to berried sweetness to syrupy body.

04
Wash

Milling and grading

Beans are dried, hulled, and sorted by size and density to preserve consistency lot by lot.

05
Qolo

Roasting and export

Roast curves are matched to origin so Yirgacheffe stays lifted while Guji keeps its chocolate depth.

06
Q-grader review

Cupping and quality control

Lots are cupped multiple times before release to keep defects low and clarity high.

07
Final journey

Packaging and shipping

Traditional pattern cues meet modern freshness protection and traceability notes.
